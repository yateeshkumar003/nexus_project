Steps Involved in Writing and Publishing a Case Report

  1. Select an interesting / rare clinical case.
  2. Obtain patient consent and institutional approval.
  3. Review CARE guidelines and relevant literature.
  4. Draft Introduction, Case Description, Discussion & Conclusions.
  5. Edit for journal style, create figures / tables.
  6. Short-list journals and format per instructions.
  7. Submit, respond to peer-review, and publish.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Case Report?

A case report is a detailed description of a single patient’s presentation, diagnosis, treatment, and outcome—usually highlighting unusual or novel aspects that enrich medical literature.

Why is it Important?

  • Shares rare or novel clinical scenarios.
  • Generates hypotheses for future original research.
  • Enhances clinicians’ diagnostic and therapeutic knowledge.
  • Provides an entry point for students and early-career doctors to publish.

Does a Case Report Add Value to Your CV?

Yes. It demonstrates analytical ability, scientific writing, and familiarity with the publication process. Credentialing bodies worldwide (USMLE, UK specialty recruitment, AMC, Indian promotions) award explicit points for published case reports.
