What is a Narrative Review?

A narrative review summarises the current knowledge on a topic, highlighting key findings from existing literature in an accessible way.

Key Features of Our Programme

  • Two cohorts each month · start 15th
  • Small group: 4–6 students
  • Live & recorded sessions · 1 : 1 mentor support
  • Fast turnaround: 2–3 weeks · 4–6 h/week commitment
  • Authorship assigned strictly by ICMJE criteria

Who Can Join?

  • Medical under-graduates, interns, or graduates
  • Beginners welcome—no prior publications required
  • Advanced learners seeking CV-boosting output

How to Register & What Happens Next

  1. Pick a fee link below and complete payment (INR shows; local currency appears at checkout).
  2. Within 24 h you’ll receive an email with the group-join link (check spam).
  3. Group workflow:
    • Introductions & topic finalisation.
    • Live / recorded class on executing the review and using research tools.
    • Task allocation with clear deadlines; mentor answers queries in-app.
    • Draft reviewed for grammar, language & plagiarism.
    • Journal shortlist → documentation & submission guidance.

Publication Notes

  • Publication fees (if any) are split equally among student authors.
  • TGRP supports the article until acceptance in an indexed journal (PubMed preferred; Scopus/DOAJ alternatives).
